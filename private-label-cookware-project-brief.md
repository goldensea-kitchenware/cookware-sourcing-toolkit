## Technical & Commercial Context: Private Label Sourcing

For a successful private label (OEM/ODM) cookware launch, buyers must detail both branding aesthetics and manufacturing logistics:

### 1. Logo Application Techniques
* **Bottom Stamping (钢印):** A mechanical press stamps the logo into the bottom metal sheet (exterior SS430 layer) before or during the cladding/bonding process. Highly durable and looks premium.
* **Laser Etching (雷雕):** A laser burns the logo onto the steel surface (commonly interior wall, handle, or lid). Offers high precision for detailed logos, but lacks the depth of stamping.
* **Etched Logo (蚀刻):** Chemical etching, often used for capacity measurement markings on the interior cooking surface.
* **Handle Casting / Embossing (铸造/压花):** Logo cast directly into custom handles (requires custom molds).

### 2. Tooling Molds & Ownership
* If your private label project requires a unique handle design, helper handle, or non-standard vessel size, you must invest in custom tooling.
* *Sourcing standard:* Custom tooling paid for by the buyer must remain the **sole property of the buyer**. The supplier is prohibited from using these molds for other customers.
* *Amortization clause:* Negotiate a mold fee refund threshold (e.g., supplier refunds $100\%$ of the mold charge once the buyer places cumulative orders exceeding 5,000 units of that SKU).

### 3. Packaging & Box Finishes
* **Retail Color Box:** Typically constructed from high-density paperboard (e.g., $350\text{g} - 400\text{g}$ CCNB bonded to E-flute corrugated cardboard) to prevent crushing.
* **Box Finishes:** Specify surface finishes such as **Matte Lamination** or **Glossy Lamination**, **Spot UV** highlights for the brand name, and **Foil Stamping** (Gold/Silver) for premium collections.
* **Transit Protection:** Specify master cartons made of **double-wall corrugated cardboard (BC-flute)** to resist moisture and high stacking pressures during ocean transit. Ensure packaging is verified against **ISTA 3A** or similar drop test protocols.

## 10. Supplier Discussion Questions

1. *Which of your standard cookware bodies can accommodate our handle design without requiring new body tooling?*
2. *What is the mold design time, sample tooling lead time, and mass production tooling lead time for custom handles?*
3. *What is the maximum weight capacity of your master cartons, and do you perform ISTA drop tests in-house?*
4. *Do you work with global non-stick coating brands like Whitford or Greblon? Can we request specific coatings?*
5. *Can you provide a draft of your social audit report (BSCI/SMETA) and ISO certificate for our compliance officer's review?*
